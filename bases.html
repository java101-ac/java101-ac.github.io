Bases

Exercises

1. Which of the following lists of bases corresponds to the following:

    Binary, Decimal, Octal, Hexadecimal

    A. 2, 10, 8, 16
    B. 2, 9, 8, 6
    C. 1, 10, 5, 6
    D. 2, 10, 3, 16

2. Convert 10101102 to decimal and hexadecimal

    A. Decimal: 86      Hex: 56
    B. Decimal: 94      Hex: 5E
    C. Decimal: 71      Hex: 47
    D. Decimal: 53      Hex: 35

3. Which of the following are true?

    I. Most computers run on base 2
    II. Hexadecimal can store more data in less space than decimal
    III. Any base can be converted into another
    IV. The range of digits allowed in a base-n system is 0 to n, inclusive

    A. I, II, and III only
    B. II, III, and IV only
    C. I, II, III, and IV only
    D. I and IV only

4. Convert 12310 to binary and hexadecimal

    A. Binary: 1111011      Hex: 7B
    B. Binary: 1101111      Hex: 6F
    C. Binary: 1011111      Hex: 5F
    D. Binary: 1001101      Hex: 4D

5. Convert 1CA16 to decimal and binary

    A. Decimal: 458      Binary: 111001010
    B. Decimal: 437      Binary: 110110101
    C. Decimal: 443      Binary: 110111011
    D. Decimal: 469      Binary: 111010101
